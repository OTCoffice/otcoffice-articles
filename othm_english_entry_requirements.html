These requirements must be met before enrolment, as required by OTHM Qualifications. Proof of English proficiency is a mandatory condition of registration — not a post-enrolment target.

Who must meet this requirement?

This requirement applies to all applicants who are non-native English speakers and who have not completed their final two years of secondary schooling taught entirely in English.

Native English speakers Exempt
Final two years of school taught in English Exempt
WAEC learners — schooled in English Exempt
Non-native, schooled in another language Must demonstrate B2

Frequently asked questions

Can I enrol first and then improve my English alongside my studies?

No. OTHM requires that all non-native English speakers demonstrate the required level before being accepted onto any programme. English proficiency is a pre-enrolment condition, not a concurrent target.

I completed a diploma taught in English. Am I exempt?

Not automatically. Exemption applies only to those who completed the final two years of secondary school taught in English. A diploma delivered in English does not fulfil this condition unless the subject was English language itself.

Will the university I progress to accept my English certificate?

It is your responsibility to confirm this with your receiving university or HEI. Some institutions do not accept alternative tests, so we recommend obtaining an IELTS or PTE certificate wherever possible.

How is compliance verified?

OTHM monitors compliance through its External Quality Assurance (EQA) process. Centres that do not meet these requirements risk sanctions under their terms and conditions with OTHM.
